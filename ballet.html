Ballet is a formal, theatrical dance style characterized by precise, graceful movements and gestures used to tell a story or express emotions. It combines dance with music, often incorporating costumes and scenery to create a visual and artistic experience. There are 4 main styles: Classical, Romantic, Neoclassical, Contemporary/Modern

Classical

This style adheres to the traditional ballet technique and vocabulary, emphasizing fluid movements, long lines, and a focus on narrative and storytelling through choreography.

Romantic

This style emerged in the early 19th century and focuses on emotion, drama, and storytelling. Romantic ballet often features supernatural or fantastical storylines, emphasizing the dancer's ability to express a range of emotions.

Neoclassical

This style emerged in the 20th century, influenced by the work of George Balanchine. Neoclassical ballet emphasizes athleticism, speed, and impressive technical feats, often with a focus on abstract or non-narrative themes.

Contemporary/Modern

This style is characterized by its experimentation and creativity, drawing inspiration from other dance styles like modern and jazz. Contemporary ballet may incorporate modern approaches to music and performance, focusing on the power of movement and its ability to express a range of emotions
