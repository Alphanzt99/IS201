Folk Dance

Giving the past, present, and future meaning and life!

Posted by Start Bootstrap on August 24, 2023

Folk dance is a type of dance that is vernacular, typically recreational, and reflects the culture of a particular region or country. It's often passed down through generations within a specific community and is often associated with celebrations, rituals, or social gatherings.

In a world that is so diverse and complex, Folk dance allows for people outside of a country's cultural sphere of influence to get a glimps of the history, values, and characteristics of a people and place.

Types of Dance

While there are as many folk dances as there are ethnic groups and cultures, there are some dances that have grown univerally popular due to a variety of reasons They include:

  • Contra/Square Dancing: These are popular American folk dances, often performed at social events and gatherings.
  • Clogging: A lively folk dance originating from Appalachian regions, known for its rhythmic footwork. Is similar to Tap Dance, but has distinct differences.
  • Polka: A lively and energetic folk dance from the Czech Republic, known for its cheerful music and lively steps.
  • Fandago: A traditional Spanish dance, often accompanied by music and hand-clapping.
  • Tarantella: An Italian folk dance known for its quick steps and upbeat rhythm.
  • Tinikling: A national dance of the Philippines, where dancers move between bamboo poles.
  • Hora: A popular Jewish wedding dance, but also danced in other countries like Romania, Bulgaria, and Israel.
