Jazz

The style of dance that rocked the 1900s

Posted by Start Bootstrap on August 24, 2023

Jazz dance is characterized by its upbeat tempo, energetic movements, improvisation, and a strong connection to the music's rhythm and syncopation. It also features isolations, where specific body parts are moved independently, and a focus on expressing personality and individuality.

It emerged from African American vernacular dances of the late 1800s and early 1900s, heavily influenced by the rise of jazz music and the rhythms of African traditions. It evolved from social dances like the Charleston and Lindy Hop into a recognized performance art, incorporating elements of ballet, modern dance, and other styles

Vernacular Jazz

A style of dance that developed organically within a specific community, rather than being formally choreographed or taught. These dances are often learned informally and are deeply rooted in the everyday culture and traditions of the community.hese dances include the Lindy Hop, Tap, Charleston, Black Bottom, Cakewalk etc.

Broadway/Dramatic Jazz

A dynamic, theatrical dance style blending traditional jazz technique with storytelling, character development, and dramatic choreography. It features intricate footwork, expressive gestures, and dynamic staging, embodying characters and narratives from musical theater through movement.
